Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water

"Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources"

Sustainable Development Goal 14 is about "Life below water" and is one of the 17 Sustainable Development Goals established by the United Nations in 2015. The official wording is to "Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development". This SDG is essential as it helps to reduce marine pollution, protects and restores ecosystems, minimizes acidity in the oceans, promotes sustainable fishing, conserves coastal and marine areas, and increases economic benefits from the sustainable use of marine resources.

ProjectShowcase

#1 Reduce Marine Pollution

Reducing marine pollution entails planning a cleanup project for rivers, oceans, or coastal areas. It may help minimize trash, particularly plastic bags. These also includes launching a campaign to raise awareness about the consequences of plastic on the oceans and implementing waste and wastewater management methods.

#2 Protect and Restore Ecosystems

Protecting and restoring ecosystems entails supporting small-scale producers by purchasing local fish. This includes applying science-based management approaches to restore fish stocks.

#3 Conserve coastal and marine areas

This includes preserving at least 10% of coastal and marine areas and assisting firms that build undersea weather stations to monitor water.
